Cross product

Abstract

We shall briefly explain the cross product of two vectors.

$\require{ams}\require{physics}$

Definitions

Suppose we have two column vectors $ \va{a}=[a_i]$ and $\va{b}=[b_i] $ for $ i=1,2,3 $. We define the cross product of $ \va{a},\va{b} $ by

\[ \va{a} \times \va{b} = \begin{bmatrix} a_2 b_3 - a_3 b_2 \\a_3 b_1 - a_1 b_3 \\ a_1 b_2 - a_2 b_1 \end{bmatrix} \]

Mnemonics

By extending the rule of Sarrus for determinant of $2 \times 2$ matrix, we have the following diagram.

\begin{matrix} a_1 & & a_2 & & a_3 & & a_1 \\ & \large\times_3 & & \large\times_1 & & \large\times_2 \\ b_1 & & b_2 & & b_3 & & b_1 \\ \end{matrix}

By multiplying a pair diagonally and taking difference between them, we can calculate each row of the cross product. For example, the first row of the cross product $ \va{a} \times \va{b} $ can be obtainded by

\begin{matrix} a_2 & & a_3 & \\ & \large\times_1 & & = a_2\; b_3 - a_3\; b_2 = \text{the first row of }\va{a} \times \va{b} \\ b_2 & & b_3 & \\ \end{matrix}

You have to remenber the index of times symbol which indicate the order of multiplication i.e. the row number.
